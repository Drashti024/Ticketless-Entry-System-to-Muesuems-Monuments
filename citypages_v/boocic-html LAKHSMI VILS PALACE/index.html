LAKHSMI VILAS PALACE

About Place

The Lakshmi Vilas Palace in Vadodara, Gujarat, India, was constructed by the Gaekwad family, a prominent Maratha family, who ruled the Baroda State. Major Charles Mant was credited to be the main architect of the palace.[1][2] Lakshmi Vilas Palace was styled on the Indo-Saracenic Revival architecture, built by Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III in 1890 at a cost of £180,000 (₹27,00,000).

HOW TO REACH THERE

 If you are travelling to Vadodra in a private vehicle, you may drive along National Highway No. 8 (NH 8) passing through Vadodra city. There are AC and Non AC buses and other affordable public transport facilities.

 

 High–speed trains such as Shatabdi Express, Rajdhani Express and other Superfast trains halt at Vadodara Junction station. So, if you are planning via railways, you can check the train schedule and book tickets as per your convenience.

 

  Vadodra has extensive connectivity with most of the major Indian cities. These include Mumbai, Ahmedabad, Delhi, Bangalore etc. So, you may book your flight tickets accordingly.
